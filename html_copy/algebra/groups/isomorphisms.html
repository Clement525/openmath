Group Isomorphism
An isomorphism from a group \( \left( G, \star \right) \) to a group \( \left( H, \cdot \right) \) is a bijective function \( \phi : G \to H \) such that for any \( a, b \in G \) : \[ \phi \left( a \star b \right) = \phi \left( a \right) \cdot \phi \left( b \right) \]
Isomorphic Groups
If there is an isomorphism from \( G \) to \( H \) we say that \( G \) and \( H \) are isomorphic and write \( G \cong H \)
Identities are Carried
Let \( e _ G \) be the identity of \( G \) and \( e _ H \) of \( H \), then for any isomorphism from \( G \) to \( H \), we have \[ \phi \left( e _ G \right) = e _ H \]

We know \( e _ G = e _ G e _ G \), therefore \( \phi \left( e _ H \right) = \phi \left( e _ H e _ H \right) = \phi \left( e _ H \right) \phi \left( e _ H \right) \), but also \( \phi \left( e _ H \right) = e _ G \phi \left( e _ H \right) \) since \( e _ G \) is the identity, by right cancellation this yields \( e _ G = \phi \left( e _ G \right) \) as needed.

Powers Pass Through a Homomorphism
Let \( \left( G, \star _ G \right) \) and \( \left( H, \star _ H \right) \) be two groups and \( \phi \) a homomorphism between them. Let \( n \in \mathbb{ Z } \) and let \( g \in G \) be any group element, then we have \[ \phi \left( g ^ n \right) = \phi \left( g \right) ^ n \]

We first show it for all \( n \in \mathbb{ N } _ 1 \) via induction. For \( n = 1 \) we can see that \( \phi \left( g ^ 1 \right) = \phi \left( g \right) ^ 1 \) trivially holds.

Ho we let \( k \in \mathbb{ N } _ 1 \) and assume that the statement holds for \( k \) we'll now show it must hold for \( k + 1 \), to do this we note the following : \[ \begin{align} \phi \left( g ^ { k + 1 } \right) &= \phi \left( g ^ k \star _ G g \right) \\ &= \phi \left( g ^ k \right) \star _ H \phi \left( g \right) \\ &= \phi \left( g \right) ^ k \star _ H \phi \left( g \right) \\ & = \phi \left( g \right) ^ { k + 1 } \end{align} \] Where we've used our induction hypothesis going from the 2nd to the 3rd line.

The above holds us over for \( \mathbb{ N } _ 1 \), but it turns out we can now extend this to \( \mathbb{ Z } ^ - \) quite easily, given some \( z \in \mathbb{ Z } ^ - \) we first note that \( e _ H = \phi \left( e _ G \right) = \phi \left( g ^ z \star _ G g ^ { -z } \right) = \phi \left( g ^ z \right) \star _ H \phi \left( g \right) ^ { -z } \), note that we justify the last equality because \( -z \in \mathbb{ N } _ 1 \) and then use our previous paragraph's proof. Finally by multiplying on the right by \( \phi \left( g \right) ^ z \) then we obtain \( \phi \left( g \right) ^ z = \phi \left( g ^ z \right) \) as needed.

We could stop there and we've left nothing out. Well indeed we have left nothing out, that is we've left \( 0 \) out, well if \( \phi \left( g ^ 0 \right) = \phi \left( e _ G \right) = e _ H = \phi \left( g \right) ^ 0 \) as needed.

Powers pass through an Isomorphism
Let \( \left( G, \star _ G \right) \) and \( \left( H, \star _ H \right) \) be two groups and \( \phi \) a isomorphism between them. Let \( n \in \mathbb{ Z } \) and let \( g \in G \) be any group element, then we have \[ \phi \left( g ^ n \right) = \phi \left( g \right) ^ n \]
We know that an isomorphism is a homomorphism, therefore powers pass through it
