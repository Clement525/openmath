Suppose we have an undirected graph \( G = (V, E) \) where \( |V| = n \) and \( |E| = m \). Assume \( G \) is connected and we want to delete some edges such that the resulting graph is disconnected. Give a \( \mathcal{O} (m \cdot n^2) \)-time algorithm that returns the minimum number of edges to delete to make the graph disconnected. Briefly explain why your algorithm works.
