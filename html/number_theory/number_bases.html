base represenation
Suppose that \( m , k \in \mathbb{N}_{1} \) and \( \left ( a_{k - 1} , a_{k - 2} , \ldots , a_{0} \right ) \in \left \lbrace 0 , 1 , 2 , \ldots , m \right \rbrace^{k} \). Then we define \( n := \sum_{i = 0}^{k - 1} a_{i} m^{i} \) and we say that \( a_{k - 1} a_{k - 2} \ldots a_{1} \) is the representation of \( n \) in the base \( m \)
decimal representation
The decimal representation of a number \( n \) is the represetnation of \( n \) in the base \( 10 \)
The decimal representation of a number is the usual way you've most likely dealt with numbers so far, for example when you write \( 123 \) you are referring to the number \( 1 \cdot 10^{2} + 2 \cdot 10^{1} + 3 \cdot 10^{0} \)
binary representation
The binary representation of a number \( n \) is the represetnation of \( n \) in the base \( 2 \)
division by 9 rule
Suppose that \( a_{k - 1} a_{k - 2} \ldots a_{1} \) is the decimal representation of \( n \), then \( 9 | n \) if and only if \( 9 | \sum_{i = 0}^{k - 1} a_{i} \)
