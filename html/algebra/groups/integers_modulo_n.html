Congruence Equivalence Classes Notation
With \( n \in \mathbb{N}_{1} \) then the relation \( a R b \) defined as \( a \% n = b \% n \) induces an equivalence relation therefore given any \( a \in \mathbb{Z} \) we get an equivalence class \( \left [ a \right ] \), and we define \( \overline{a} := \left [ a \right ] \) to lighten the standard notation of equivalence classes
The Integers Modulo \( n \)
Suppose that \( n \in \mathbb{N}_{1} \), then we define \[ \mathbb{ Z } _ n ^ \% := \left \lbrace \overline{0} , \overline{1} , \overline{2} , \ldots , \overline{n - 1} \right \rbrace \] and call this set the integers modulo \( n \)
Addition in \( \mathbb{ Z } _ n ^ \% \)
Suppose that \( a , b \in \mathbb{Z} \), then we define \( \overline{a} + \overline{b} = \overline{a + b} \)
Multiplication in \( \mathbb{ Z } _ n ^ \% \)
Suppose that \( a , b \in \mathbb{Z} \), then we define \( \overline{a} \cdot \overline{b} = \overline{a \cdot b} \)
choice of representative doesn't matter for addition
