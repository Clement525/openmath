Linear Transformation
A linear transformation from \( V \) to \( W \) is a function \( T : V \to W \) with the following properties for any \( u, v \in V \) and \( c \in F \)
  • \( T \left( u + v \right) = T \left( u \right) + T \left( v \right) \)
  • \( T \left( c u \right) = c T \left( u \right) \)
Inner Product
An inner product is a function \( \left\langle \cdot , \cdot \right\rangle : V \times V \to F \) where \( V \) is a vector space over the field \( F \) satisfying
  • Conjugate Symmetry: \( \left\langle x, y \right\rangle = \overline{ \left\langle y, x \right\rangle } \)
  • Linearity: \( \left\langle a x + by, z \right\rangle = a \left\langle x, z \right\rangle + b \left\langle y, z \right\rangle \)
  • Positive Definite: For any \( x \in V ^ { \neq 0 } \) \( \left\langle x, x \right\rangle \gt 0 \)
