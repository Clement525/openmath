vectors

dot product
Given two sequences of numbers of the same length: `a = (a_1, ..., a_k)` and `b = (b_1, ..., b_k)`, their dot product denoted and defined by by `a * b = sum_{i = 1}^k a_i * b_i`. Note that `* : RR^k xx RR^k to RR`
vector form of a line
Suppose that `d, p` are vectors, then we say that the set `l := {x : x = td + p, text( for some ) t in RR}` is a line and say that the vector equation for the line is
`x = td + p`
We also say that `d` is the direction vector for `l`

An example of the vector form of a line in `RR^3` could be `x = t [[1], [2], [3]] + [[3], [2], [1]]`, where `[[3], [2], [1]]` (the red dot) is a part of the line when `t = 0`, and the line's direction follows the direction of the vector `[[1], [2], [3]]`
