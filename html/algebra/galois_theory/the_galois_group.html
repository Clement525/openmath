Automorphism
If \( E \) is a field, then an automorphism of \( E \) is an isomorphism of \( E \) with itself.
Automorphism Fixed Pointwise
If \( E / F \) is a field extension, then an automorphism \( \sigma \) of \( E \) fixes \( F \) pointwise if \( \sigma \left( c \right) = c \) for every \( c \in F \)

Suppose that the degree of the extension \( E / F \) is a prime \( p \). Show that any subfields \( K \) of \( E \) containing \( F \) is either \( E \) or \( F \).
Suppose that \( K \) is a subfield of \( E \) containing \( F \), we know that \( \left[ E : F \right] = p \), but also that \[ p = \left[ E: F \right] = \left[ E : K \right] \left[ K : F \right] \] Since \( p \) is prime then we have two cases, if \( \left[ E : K \right] = 1, \left[ K : F \right] = p \) then we know \( K = E \), in the other case we will obtain that \( F = K \), which proves what we wanted to show.

Polynomials as Matrices
Let \( p \left( x \right) = a_0 + a_1 x ^ 1 + \ldots + a_m x ^ m \in F \left[ x \right] \) be a polynomial with coefficients in a field \( F \). Let \( A \in M _ { n \times n } \left( F \right) \), and note that we can evaluate \( p \left( A \right) = a_0 I + a_1 A + \ldots + a_m A ^ m \)
  • Prove that there is a non-zero polynomial \( p \left( x \right) \in F \left[ x \right] \) such that \( p \left( A \right) = 0 \)
  • Let \( I \) be the set of all polynomaisl \( q \left( x \right) \in F \left[ x \right] \) such that \( q \left( A \right) = 0 \), prove that \( I \) is an ideal in \( F \left[ x \right] \)
  • Let \( m \left( x \right) \) be a monic polynomilal of least degree in \( I \). Prove that \( I = \left( m \left( x \right) \right) _ \diamond \).
  • Prove that \( m \left( x \right) \) is the unique monic polynomial of least degree in \( I \)

Consider \( M _ { n \times n } \left( F \right) \) as a vector space over \( F \), in this case it's easy to see that the "one-hot" matricies at each \( i, j \) form a basis for this vector space, and the dimension is precisely \( n ^ 2 \).

On the other hand, supposing we had the matrices \( A ^ 0, \ldots A ^ \left( n ^ 2 \right) \), then as there are \( n ^ 2 + 1 \) of these matrices then we know that they are linearly depedent, so we get coefficients \( a _ 0, \ldots a _ \left( n ^ 2 \right) \in F\) not all zero such that \( a _ 0 A ^ 0 + \ldots + a _ { n ^ 2 } A ^ \left( n ^ 2 \right) = 0 \), semantically replace every \( A \) with an \( x \) to obtain a polynomial such that \( A \) is a root.

let \( j \left( x \right), k \left( x \right) \in I \), note that \( \left( j - k \right) \left( x \right) = j \left( x \right) - k \left( x \right) \) so that \( \left( j - k \right) \left( A \right) = 0 - 0 = 0 \) so that \( \left( j - k \right) \left( x \right) \in I \), also let \( r \left( x \right) \in F \left[ x \right] \) then \( r \left( x \right) j \left( x \right) \in I \) because \( r \left( A \right) j \left( A \right) = r \left( A \right) \cdot 0 = 0 \), so that \( I \) is an ideal.

We want to show that \( I = \left( m \left( x \right) \right) _ \diamond \), one way of proving this is to show that \( m \left( x \right) \) divides every element in \( I \). So let \( f \left( x \right) \in I \) by the quotient remainder theorem we obtain \( q \left( x \right) , r \left( x \right) \) such that \( f \left( x \right) = q \left( x \right) m \left( x \right) + r \left( x \right) \) where either \( r \left( x \right) = 0 \) or \( \operatorname{ deg } \left( r \right) \lt \operatorname{ deg } \left( m \right) \), but note the latter is impossible because \( m \left( x \right) \) was the monic polynomial with the smallest degree in \( I \) (which is also the smallest degree of any polynomial in \( I \)) so that we must have \( r \left( x \right) = 0 \) which implies that \( m \left( x \right) \) divides \( f \left( x \right) \) as needed.

Suppose that there was another monic polynomial \( c \left( x \right) \in I \) with the same degree as \( m \left( x \right) \), we know that \( m \left( x \right) \) divides \( c \left( x \right) \), but then we must have that the quotient is a non-zero constant polynomial, so that \( c \left( x \right) = \alpha m \left( x \right) \), but then \( \alpha m \left( x \right) \) is no longer monic unless \( \alpha = 1 \) therefore \( c \left( x \right) = m \left( x \right) \) as needed.
