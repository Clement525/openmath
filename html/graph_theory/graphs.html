Degree of a Vertex
Suppose that \( G = \left( V, E \right) \) is a graph, then we say that the degree of a vertex \( v \) is the number of other vertices connected to it with an edge. We define the syntax \( \operatorname{ deg } : V \to \mathbb{ N } ^ 0 \) to be the function that maps a node to it's degree.
Sum of Degrees
Suppose that \( G \) is a finite graph, then we have that \[ \sum _ { v \in V } \operatorname{ deg } \left( v \right) = 2 \lvert E \rvert \]
Branching Factor
Suppose that we have a graph \( G \), then we say that \( G \) has branching factor \( b \in \mathbb{ N } _ 1 \), if every vertex has degree \( b \)
Average Branching Factor
Suppose that \( G \) is a finite graph, then the average branching factor of the graph is defined as \[ \frac{\sum _ { v \in V } \operatorname{ deg } \left( v \right)}{\lvert V \rvert } \in \mathbb{ Q } \]
Maximum Branching Factor
Suppose that we have a graph \( G \), then we say that \( G \) has a maximum branching factor of \( b \in \mathbb{ N } _ 0 \), if every vertex has degree at most \( b \)
Path
A tuple \( \left( v _ 1, v _ 2, \ldots v _ n \right) \in V ^ n \) is said to be a path if for every \( i \in [1 \ldots n - 1] \), \( \left\{ v _ i, v _ { i + 1 } \right\} \in E \).
Connected Vertices
Suppose that \( u, v \in V\), then we say that \( u, v \) are connected if there is a path \( v _ 1, v _ 2, \ldots, v _ n \) where \( v _ 1 = u \) and \( v _ n = v \)
Connected Graph
Given a graph \( G \) we say that it is connected when for every \( u, v \in V \), we have that \( u \) and \( v \) are connected.
