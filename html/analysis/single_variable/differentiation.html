derivative
Let \( f \) be a function and let \( a \) be a point in its domain, then the derivative of f at a is defined as: \( \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f{\left ( a + h \right )} - f{\left ( a \right )}}{h} \), when it exists and is denoted by \( f^{'}{\left ( a \right )} \) or \( \frac{d}{dx} f{\left ( a \right )} \)
partial derivative
Let \( U \) be an open subset of \( \mathbb{R}^{n} \), and let \( f{:} U \to \mathbb{R} \) be a function, then the partial derivative with respect to the \( i \)-th variable, \( x_{i} \), of \( f \) at \( a = \left ( a_{1} , \ldots , a_{n} \right ) \in U \) is defined as: \( \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f{\left ( a_{1} , \ldots , a_{i} + h , \ldots , a_{n} \right )} - f{\left ( a_{1} , \ldots , a_{i} , \ldots , a_{n} \right )}}{h} \) when it exists, and is denoted by \( \frac{\partial}{\partial x_{i}} f{\left ( a \right )} \)
