upper bound
Given \( S \subseteq P \) where \( P \) is a partial order, then \( u \in P \) is said to be an upper bound iff \( \forall s \in S , s \le u \). we say that \( S \) is bounded above by \( u \)
lower bound
Given \( S \subseteq P \) where \( P \) is a partial order , then \( l \in P \) is said to be an lower bound iff \( \forall s \in S , l \le s \), in this case we say that \( S \) is bounded below by \( l \).
bounded
A set \( S \subseteq P \) where \( P \) is a partial order, then \( P \) is said to be bounded if it is bounded above and bounded below
bounded function
we say that \( f{:} X \to R \) is bounded on \( S \subseteq X \) when \( f{\left ( S \right )} \) is bounded
Infimum
The infimum of a subset \( S \) of a partially ordered set \( P \) is the greatest element in \( P \) that is a lower bound of \( S \) and is denoted by \( \text{inf} \left ( S \right ) \). Symbolically this means that \( \forall s \in S , s \ge \text{inf} \left ( S \right ) \), and if \( k \) is a lower bound, then \( k \le \text{inf} \left ( S \right ) \)
Supremum
The supremum of a subset \( S \) of a partially ordered set \( P \) is the least element in \( P \) that is a upper bound of \( S \) and is denoted by \( \text{sup} \left ( S \right ) \). Symbolically this means that \( \forall s \in S , s \le \text{sup} \left ( S \right ) \), and if \( k \) is a upper bound, then \( k \ge \text{sup} \left ( S \right ) \)
greater than inf implies not a lower bound
Suppose that \( \text{inf} \left ( S \right ) \lt x \), then \( x \) is not an lower bound of \( S \)

Suppose that \( \text{inf} \left ( S \right ) \lt x \), then for the sake of contradiction assume that \( x \) is a lower bound of \( S \), then \( \text{inf} \left ( S \right ) \ge x \) which is a contradiction.

less than sup implies not an upper bound
if \( x \lt \text{sup} \left ( S \right ) \), then \( x \) is not a upper bound of \( S \)

Suppose that \( \text{sup} \left ( S \right ) \gt x \), then for the sake of contradiction assume that \( x \) is a upper bound of \( S \), then \( \text{sup} \left ( S \right ) \le x \) which is a contradiction.

least upper bound property
We say that a partially ordered set \( X \) has the least upper bound property if every non-empty subset of \( X \) that has an upper bound, has a least upper bound in \( X \). Symbolically that is, if \( S \subseteq X \) and \( S \ne \emptyset \), if \( S \) has an upper bound, then \( \text{sup} \left ( S \right ) \in S \)
