Equicontinuous at a Point
Let \( \mathcal{ F } \) be a family of functions of the form \( S \to \mathbb{ R } ^ m \) where \( S \subseteq \mathbb{ R } ^ n \), then we say that \( \mathcal{ F } \) is equicontinuous at a point \( a \in S \) if for every \( \epsilon \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \) there exists a \( \delta \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \) such that for all \( x \in S \) and \( f \in \mathcal{ F } \) \[ \lVert x - a \rVert \lt \delta \implies \lVert f \left( x \right) - f \left( a \right) \rVert \lt \epsilon \]
Equicontinuous Family
We say that \( \mathcal{ F } \) is an equicontinuous family of functions of the form \( S \to \mathbb{ R } \) diff for every \( a \in S \) we have that \( \mathcal{ F } \) is is equicontinuous at \( a \).
Uniformly Equicontinuous Family
Let \( \mathcal{ F } \) be a family of functions of the form \( S \to \mathbb{ R } ^ m \) where \( S \subseteq \mathbb{ R } ^ n \), then we say that \( \mathcal{ F } \) is uniformly equicontinuous if for every \( \epsilon \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \) there exists a \( \delta \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \) such that for all \( x, y \in S \) and \( f \in \mathcal{ C } \) \[ \lVert x - y \rVert \lt \delta \implies \lVert f \left( x \right) - f \left( y \right) \rVert \lt \epsilon \]
Compact Subsets of Continuous Functions with Compact Domain is Equicontinuous
Let \( K \) be a compact subset of \( \mathbb{ R } ^ n \), a compact subset \( \mathcal{ F } \) of \( C \left( K, \mathbb{ R } ^ m \right) \) is equicontinuous
An Equicontinuous Family of Functions whose Domain is Compact is also Uniformly Equicontinuous
If \( \mathcal{ F } \) is an equicontinuous family of functions of the form \( K \to \mathbb{ R } ^ m \) where \( K \) is compact, then \( \mathcal{ F } \) is uniformly equicontinuous
Totally Bounded
We say that a subset \( S \subseteq \mathbb{ R } ^ m \) is totally bounded if for any \( \epsilon \in \mathbb{ R } ^ + \) there exists \( a _ 1, \ldots , a _ n \) such that \[ S \subseteq \bigcup _ { i = 1 } ^ { n } B \left( a _ i, \epsilon \right) \]
A Bounded subset of Rm is Totally Bounded
Suppose that \( S \subseteq \mathbb{ R } ^ m \) is bounded, then \( S \) is totally bounded.
