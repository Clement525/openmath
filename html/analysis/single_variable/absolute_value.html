absolute value
the absolute value function \( \left \lvert \cdot \right \rvert : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}^{\ge 0} \) is defined by
\( \left | x \right | = \left \lbrace \begin{matrix} x \text{ if } x \ge 0 \\ - x \text{ if } x < 0 \end{matrix} \right . \)
absolute value differs by sign
\( \forall x \in \mathbb{R} , x = \left | x \right | \vee x = - \left | x \right | \)
Let \( x \in \mathbb{R} \) if \( x > 0 \), then \( x = \left | x \right | \) by definition, if \( x < 0 \), then \( \left | x \right | = - x \), or rather \( x = - \left | x \right | \)
multiplication in absolute value
\( \left \lvert x \cdot y \mid = \right \rvert x \left \lvert \cdot \mid y \right \rvert \)
  • If both \( x , y \) are positive then \( \left | x \cdot y \right | = x \cdot y = \left | x \right | \cdot \left | y \right | \)
  • if both \( x , y \) are negative, then their product is positive so it follows as above
  • if one of \( x , y \) is positive and the other negative, without loss of generality assume that \( x < 0 \) and \( y > 0 \)then their product is negative so \( \left | x \cdot y \right | = - 1 \cdot x \cdot y = \left | x \right | \cdot \left | y \right | \)
  • if any one of \( x , y \) is zero, then \( \left | x \cdot y \right | = 0 = \left | x \right | \cdot \left | y \right | \)
absolute value greater inequality
Suppose that \( x \in \mathbb{R} \) and that \( c \in \mathbb{R} \) such that \( \left | c \right | \ge 1 \) then \( \left | x \right | \le \left | c x \right | \)
Since \( 1 \lt \left | c \right | \) then \( 1 \cdot \left | x \right | \le \left | c \right | \cdot \left | x \right | = \left | c x \right | \)
absolute value equality
Suppose that \( a , b \in \mathbb{Z} \) and that \( \left | a \right | = \left | b \right | \), then \( a = b \) or \( a = - b \)

changing sign doesn't change absolute value
\( \forall x \in \mathbb{R} , | x \left \lvert = \mid - x \right \rvert \)
Let \( x \in \mathbb{R} \) then \( \left | - x \right | = \left | - 1 \cdot x \right | = \left | - 1 \right | \cdot \left | x \right | = 1 \cdot \left | x \right | = \left | x \right | \)
absolute value is greater than itself
\( \forall x \in \mathbb{R} , x \le \left \lvert x \right \rvert \)
  • if \( x > 0 \) then \( \left | x \right | = x \)
  • if \( x < 0 \) then \( \left | x \right | = - x \) so \( \left | x \right | \ge - x > x \) this is true since \( x < 0 \) and \( 0 < - x \)
