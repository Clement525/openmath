directional derivative
Let \( f{:} \mathbb{R}^{n} \to \mathbb{R} \) and let \( a , v \in \mathbb{R}^{n} \), then the directional derivative of \( f \) at \( a \) along \( v \) is defined as
\( \nabla_{v} f{\left ( a \right )} := \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f{\left ( a + h v \right )} - f{\left ( a \right )}}{h} \)
Partial Derivative
Let \( f{:} \mathbb{R}^{n} \to R \) then the partial derivative with respect to \( x_{i} \) is the directional derivative of \( f \) at \( a \) along \( e_{i} \)
\( \frac{\partial}{\partial x_{i}} f{\left ( a \right )} := \nabla_{e_{i}} f{\left ( a \right )} \)
Frozen Function
Suppose that \( f{:} \mathbb{R}^{n} \to \mathbb{R} \) and \( a \in \mathbb{R}^{n} \) then we can define a new function \( f_{i , a}{:} \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R} \) by \( f_{i , a}{\left ( h \right )} := f{\left ( a + h e_{i} \right )} \).

The frozen function get's it's name because it can be thought of by taking the sample point \( a \) and then freezing in all directions except for along the \( i \)-th component of the input for \( f \)

Partial Derivative becomes frozen function derivative
\( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_{i}} \left ( a \right ) = \frac{d}{d h} \left [ f_{i , a}{\left ( h \right )} \right ] \)

differentaibility
a function \( f{:} A \to \mathbb{R}^{m} \) with \( A \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n} \) is differentiable at the point \( a \in \mathbb{R}^{n} \) if there exists a linear transformation \( T : \mathbb{R}^{n} \to \mathbb{R}^{m} \) such that
\( \lim_{x \to a} \frac{\left \| f{\left ( x \right )} - \left ( f{\left ( a \right )} + T \left ( x - a \right ) \right ) \right \| }{\left \| x - a \right \| } = 0 \)
continuous partials implies differentiability
If all the partial derivatives of a function exist in a neighborhood of a point \( x_{0} \) and are continuous at the point \( x_{0} \), then the function is differentiable at that point \( x_{0} \)
